Our account of the transformation from factory to trade park.

When arriving on site we were immediately struck by its potential.

This site had been available to purchase for several years yet it had been overlooked by many potential investors and developers over that period. Being a Llanelli based company we had been aware that the site was for sale however, like a large number of passers by we had not really achknowledged the old Avon Inflatables site. It was overgrown and had been derelict for years.

However in early 2014 Robert Rees, director and CEO of the Rees Group, organised to view the site. Upon arrival it was clear that this was not something that could be overlooked any longer. The old factory was the perfect project.

Sitting on 2.74 acres of land situated just 4.3 miles from junction 48 of the M4 the factory featured an ideal location with more than enough land to play with. The factory itself offers 35,000 square feet of open floor space, with nothing but steel pillars in the building. It was any developers dream yet no one had realised it.

Once on site we did not hold back. The doors were opened and we moved in.

Once the sale of the site had been completed we were given the keys. We wasted no time and immediately went down to the old factory. We were excited, energetic and enthusiatic all at the same time and could not wait to start work on the site. The Rees Group will transform this into a premier trade park location.

As well as potential on the inside the site also has huge potential on the outside. Sitting on 2.74 acres of land the old factory will be converted into a logistical haven. When cleared the area behind the building will open up the potential for future developements such as additioanl trade units, storage areas, overflow car parking space or even loading and unloading bays for arctic lorries. All of these uses seriously benefiting from the sites location.

We began work on the outside of the site almost immediately after recieiving the keys, working tirelessly to clear the overgrowth that had developed over the years. We sent in the strimmers, mowers and chainsaws and within 3 weeks the land was clear. It was a tough job but we had a strong team who made it happen.

After the inital surveys we began to clear any debris that was left within the building. This included any equipment that had been left within the factory from its previous owners as well as any unnecessary connections. We cleared out the old office spaces discovering a number of beautiful, original draftsman sketches from its previous use as the home of Avon Inflatables. We kept these images and we hope to display them on site when the transformation is complete.

Clearing the inside of the building took very little time in comparison to the outside as the inside only required a small amount of clearing out. However during stage two of the development the inside will have a very large amount of attention. It is our goal to split the building up into a number of trade stands.

A building with many stories to tell and a rich fabrication history.

Through the knowledge of the neighbours to the factory as well as a number of Dafen locals we have been able to determine that the old Avon Inflatables site is over 60 years old and has had 3 previous uses. We were told that the building was originally constructed for use during the war time era however it was never required.
